Pocherong Baboy

Filipino Cuisine

Prep Time : 10mins

Cooking Time : 1hour 15minutes

Pocherong Baboy is a Filipino pork stew known for its rich flavors. While various pork cuts can be utilized, the belly, also known as liempo, is often preferred. At first glance, it may bear similarities to nilagang baboy, but distinctive ingredients set it apart. Notably, the inclusion of fresh tomato and tomato sauce adds a unique depth to this dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ lbs pork belly cut into cubes
  • 2 bunches baby bok choy
  • 2 medium ripe saba banana
  • 4 medium ripe tomato
  • 12 pieces long green beans
  • 1 medium potato cubed
  • 4 ounces tomato sauce
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 6 to 8 cups pork broth
  • 2 ½ tablespoons fish sauce
  • to taste ground pepper
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a cooking pot.
  2. Once the oil becomes hot, saute onion and garlic.
  3. Add pork belly. Continue to saute until it turns light brown.
  4. Add Chorizo de Bilbao. Cook for 1 minute.
  5. Put the tomato into the pot. Stir and pour-in tomato sauce. Continue to cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Pour beef or pork broth into the pot. Let boil. Cover and cook in low heat for 45 to 60 minutes. Note: you can add more broth or water if needed.
  7. Add banana and potato. Cover and cook for 8 minutes.
  8. Put the baby bok choy into the pot. Cover. Cook for 2 minutes.
  9. Season with fish sauce and ground white pepper. Stir.

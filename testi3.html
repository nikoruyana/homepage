Fried Tilapia

Fried Tilapia is a simple dish that uses tilapia seasoned with salt and fried in oil.

Ginataang Tilapia

Ginataang Tilapia is a fish with spinach cooked in coconut milk, specifically using tilapia, a fish belonging to the tilapiine cichlid tribe.

Grilled Tilapia

This Grilled Tilapia requires the fish to be wrapped securely in aluminum foil to trap the juice producing a very flavorful grilled fish with a texture similar to steamed fish.

Inaloban a Isda

Inaloban a Isda is a Filipino Maranao dish prepared with grilled tilapia served in a spicy coconut gravy infused with ginger, chillies and a local type of onion called sibujing.

Paksiw na Tilapia

Paksiw is a Filipino style of cooking, whose name means "to cook and simmer in vinegar." Common dishes bearing the term, however, can vary substantially depending on what is being cooked. You can explore different fish to cook these dish like Tilapia.

Sarciadong Tilapia

Sarciadong Tilapia is done by simmering a fried Tilapia in a sauce that is usually composed of tomatoes and onions.

Sinanglay na Tilapia

Sinanglay na Tilapia is a Filipino dish wherein fish, such as tilapia, is stuffed with onion, tomato, ginger, and garic, and then cooked in coconut milk. This dish is somewhat similar to Ginataang Tilapia. However, certain ingredients and procedure varies.

Sinigang na Tilapia

Sinigang is a popular Filipino soup dish that is made by simmering meat or fish in a sour broth. The most popular ingredient is the tamarind or sampaloc but you can amp up the sourness by adding or using tomatoes, green mangoes, guava, or even the regional fruit, the batuan or batwan.

Tilapia Escabeche

Filipino Escabeche requires the fish to be fried and cooked in vinegar.

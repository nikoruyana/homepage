Pinakbet

description

Pinakbet or Pakbet is a Filipino vegetable stew from the Ilocos region. Its name is derived from the Ilocano word pinakebbet which means "shriveled," in reference to the vegetables being sweated in the pot over low heat until "shrunken."

Ingredients

  • 3 Asian eggplants, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 300 g okra, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/3 squash or buttercup, sliced roughly 1 inch
  • 250 g pork jowl, cubed
  • 2 pcs tomatoes, diced
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp bagoong alamang
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • fish sauce
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • oil

Procedure

  1. Generously season pork with salt and freshly ground black pepper, set it aside
  2. In a pan saut√© garlic, onion and tomatoes in oil.
  3. Add pork jowls and brown evenly.
  4. Add squash and okra continuously pan frying for 2 minutes, bring the heat to low and cover for 5 minutes
  5. Add eggplants pan fry pan fry for additional 2 minutes.
  6. Add bagoong monamon give it a good gentle mix to distribute the bagoong. Pour 1/4 cup water then cover and simmer in low heat for 5 minutes until vegetables are cooked.
  7. Season with pepper add fish sauce if needed.

Pork Kilawin

Filipino Cuisine

Prep Time : 10minutes

Cooking Time : 15minutes

Pork Kilawin, also known as Kilawing Baboy, is a dish typically made with grilled pork belly. In contrast to seafood kilawin dishes, pork requires pre-cooking before being prepared this way. This is because pork does not fully cook when soaked in vinegar alone. Therefore, it's essential to cook the pork beforehand before incorporating it into the kilawin dish.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs 2 lbs. grilled pork belly
  • 1 cup cane vinegar
  • 1 medium cucumber diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon granulated white sugar
  • Red chilies
  • 1 medium onion minced
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Chop the pork belly into serving pieces. Set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, combine vinegar, salt, ground black pepper, chili, and sugar. Mix well
  3. Once the liquid boils, you can reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook the mixture for about 60 minutes, or until the pork gets nice and tender.
    Arrange the pork, cucumber, and onion in a large bowl.

  6. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve.

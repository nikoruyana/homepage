Pork Nilaga

Filipino Cuisine

Prep Time : 5 minutes

Cooking Time : 1 hour

Pork Nilaga is a traditional Filipino pork soup featuring a clear broth. In this rendition, pork belly is utilized alongside ingredients like potato, saba banana, and pechay (or baby bok choy). It's an ideal dish to savor during chilly, rainy weather, providing warmth and comfort.

Marinade Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. pork belly cut into cubes
  • 1 Knorr Pork Cube
  • 3 to 4 small potato peeled and sliced in half
  • 2 pieces ripe saba banana sliced
  • 6 to 8 ounces pechay or bok choy
  • 1 medium onion sliced
  • 6 to 7 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon whole peppercorn
  • 2 to 3 fish sauce

Instructions

  1. Pour water in a cooking pot. Let boil.
  2. Add onion and whole peppercorn. Continue to boil until the onion becomes tender.
  3. Add pork belly. Continue to saute until it turns light brown.
  4. Put the pork belly into the cooking pot. Let the water re-boil.
  5. Add the pork cube. Stir. Cover the pot and continue to cook in low heat for 45 to 60 minutes or until the pork gets tender.
  6. Add potato and saba banana. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes.
  7. Heat-up the grill. Start to grill your Filipino Style pork BBQ for 3 to 5 minutes per side until the meat is thoroughly cooked.
  8. Add the fish sauce and pechay or bok choy. Stir. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.

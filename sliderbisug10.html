Looking for a special fish recipe for an occasion? This Special Kinamatisang Bisugo Soup is tasty, warm, comforting, and easy to make.

TINOLANG BISUGO

Tinolang Isda or Fish in Ginger Broth is a type of Filipino clear soup dish wherein fish such as Bisugo is cooked in a broth composed of ginger, lemongrass and other vegetables. It is a simple dish that can be enjoyed during dinner with the entire family.

PAKSIW NA BISUGO

Paksiw na Bisugo is a Filipino dish wherein fish such as galunggong is cooked with water, vinegar, and some spices.

PINANGAT NA BISUGO

Pinangat na Bisugo. The taste is between sinigang and paksiw. Best paired with a plate of hot or newly cooked rice.

SARCIADONG BISUGO

Sarciado in Tagalog means “cooked with a thick sauce”, that is why this fish dish is accompanied with a thick chunky tomato sauce. Fish Sarciado was invented by Filipinos in search for better ways to make a dish out of left over fried fish as these leftovers are not really as appetizing as the freshly fried ones, so an ingenious approach by adding tomato sauce and eggs did made the trick by not just rehydrating the fish but as well as removing the fishy odour and adding more flavour to the leftover.

SWEET AND SOUR BISUGO

There are so many ways to enjoy this dish. The sweet, tangy, sticky sauce goes well with almost any protein, from meaty fish.

FRIED BISUGO

Fried Bisugo is a delicacy that can easily turn any meal from ordinary to extraordinary.

INIHAW NA BISUGO

if you haven’t tried grilling or eating bisugo, now is the best time to do it. Just make sure your favorite dipping sauce is by your side for the total enjoyment of the dish.

SINABAWANG BISUGO

This is Sinabawang Bisugo. This down home fish soup recipe is as simple as it is delicious. It’s white fish simmered with a mixture of ginger, onions, and tomatoes. Then seasoned only with salt, pepper and fish sauce.

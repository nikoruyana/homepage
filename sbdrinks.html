Sago at Gulaman

SAGO AT GULAMAN






Sago Gulaman is a local Filipino beverage made of tapioca pearls, water, brown sugar, gulaman, and vanilla. It's a delicious and refreshing drink that is perfect to serve on a hot summer day!









Ingredients


Gulaman

  • 3 cups water
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 pack 25g red gulaman, unflavored

Sago
  • 4-5 cups water
  • ⅓ cup sago pearls
  • 1 tsp vanilla (optional)

Juice
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 3 ⅓ cups water

Procedures


For Gulaman


  1. In a pan over low heat, add 3 cups of water and ¼ cup of brown sugar, and stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Add unflavored gulaman and stir for 3-5 mins or until frothy.
  3. Turn off the heat and pour into a glass container, and cool for 20 minutes.
  4. After gulaman solidified like jello, slice into small cubes.

For Sago


  1. In a pot over medium heat, add 4-5 cups of water (add 1 more cup if needed), and bring to a boil.
  2. Add ⅓ cup sago pearls and cook until sago pearls are tender and translucent.
  3. Using a strainer, separate the sago and hot water. Then pour cold water on the sago to avoid sticking. Set aside.


For Juice


  1. In a pan over low heat, add 1 cup of brown sugar and caramelized sugar, make sure to keep an eye and stir so the sugar does not stick to the pan.
  2. Once it is caramelized, carefully pour in 3 ⅓ cups of water and stir until it is well combined.
  3. Make sure that the caramelized sugar does not burn because it will give a bitter flavor. Allow it to cool for 20 minutes.

To Assemble the Sago't Gulaman


  1. In large glasses, add ice, gulaman, cooked sago, and juice mixture.
  2. Best served cold and individual servings.
watermelon Sweet Tea

WATERMELON SWEET TEA






Watermelon Sweet Tea – Cool off at your next barbecue with a cold glass of watermelon sweet tea! This recipe is a refreshing way to enjoy summer






Ingredients


  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 quarts filtered water
  • 6 tea bags Luzianne preferred
  • 12 cups seeded watermelon chunks
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • Fresh mint leaves optional

Procedures


  1. In a large saucepan, combine the sugar and water and bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Boil until the sugar has completely dissolved and the liquid is clear.
  3. Remove from the heat, add the tea bags to the sugar water, cover, and steep for 15 minutes.
  4. Working in batches, puree the watermelon chunks and lime juice in a blender or food processor.
  5. Strain and transfer to a gallon-sized pitcher. Remove the tea bags and add the tea to the pitcher. Do not add ice.
  6. Stir and pour into ice-filled glasses. Garnish each glass with 2 fresh mint leaves, if desired, and serve.

Calamansi Juice

CALAMANSI JUICE






Calamansi Juice is made of freshly squeezed calamondin juice and simple syrup. This Filipino-style limeade is tangy, refreshing, and a delicious way to keep cool all year long!






Ingredients


  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup calamansi juice

Procedures


  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and 1 cup of the water.
  2. Then, cook, stirring regularly, until sugar is dissolved. Remove from heat and allow to cool completely
  3. Halve the calamansi fruit and extract the juice by hand or a juicer.
  4. Using a fine-mesh sieve, strain and discard seeds and skin.
  5. In a pitcher, combine calamansi juice, simple syrup, and the remaining 3 cups of water. Stir well to combine.
  6. Chill in the refrigerator for a few hours or serve over ice.

Mango Nectar

MANGO NECTAR






Homemade mango nectar bursting with fresh mango flavor is the perfect thirst quencher! With only three natural ingredients, it's a cool and refreshing drink you'll love all Summer long.




Ingredients


  • 4 large mangoes (about 2 cups pulp)
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 cup of sugar

Procedures


  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Over medium heat, bring to a simmer, stirring regularly, until sugar is dissolved and syrup is clear and no longer cloudy. Remove from heat and allow to cool completely.
  2. Slice mangoes and scoop flesh, discarding pit and skin.
  3. In a blender or food processor, process mango until smooth and well-blended.
  4. In a pitcher, combine mango pulp and simple syrup. Stir until combined and refrigerate to chill.
  5. Serve in glasses over ice.

Melon Chiller

MELON CHILLER






Melon Chiller is a type of Filipino refreshment made with shredded cantaloupe, water, condensed milk, and ice. It is a popular street drink or samalamig usually sold alongside sago’t gulaman and buko juice, in plastic bags with straws.






Ingredients


  • 1 large cantaloupe
  • 4 cups cold water
  • 1 can (14 ounces) condensed milk
  • ice

Procedures


  1. Cut melon into half. With a spoon, scrape seeds and pulp.
  2. Place pulp and seeds in a bowl, add 1 cup of the water, and repeatedly press down with the back of a spoon to extract the juice.
  3. In a fine-mesh sieve, strain. Discard seeds and reserve the juice.
  4. Using a melon shredder, shred cantaloupe meat.
  5. In a pitcher, combine shredded cantaloupe, the reserved juice (from straining pulp), and the remaining 3 cups water.
  6. Add condensed milk and stir to disperse. Serve in glasses over ice. Consume immediately.

Buko Pandan

BUKO PANDAN






Buko pandan is a light, refreshing dessert from the Philippines that has become popular worldwide. It is deliciously creamy and subtly sweet, with a tantalizing combination of flavors derived from its two main ingredients, young coconut and pandan extract.





Ingredients


  • 1 can coconut cream (14 oz.)
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 cups shredded young coconut meat
  • 1/2 tsp pandan extract
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar

Procedures


  1. Combine the coconut cream, sugar, cornstarch, and water in a large pot. Cook over medium heat. Constantly stir until the mixture thickens and boils.
  2. If you’re using pandan extract, add it to the mixture and stir. Otherwise, add the leaves to the mixture. Boil them, then lower the heat and simmer for about 10 minutes. Remove the pandan leaves from the mixture.
  3. Remove the pot from the heat. Then, let it cool slightly. After this, stir in the young coconut meat and mix well.
  4. Pour the mixture into a 9×9 inch square dish. Then, refrigerate it for at least 4 hours or until firm.
  5. Next, beat the heavy cream and powdered sugar until stiff peaks form. Spread the cream over the chilled buko pandan. Mix well.
  6. Garnish with toasted coconut flakes or fresh fruits, if desired.glasses over ice. Consume immediately.
  7. Feel free to adjust the sugar and experiment by adding condensed milk or different fruits.
  8. Serve chilled, and enjoy your delicious buko pandan!

Buko Salad Drink

BUKO SALAD DRINK






Mouth-watering coconut juice mixed with milk, gulaman, tapioca, and coconut meat- Buko Salad Drink is so refreshing and invigorating! A pitcher of this sweet and milky drink is a perfect way to quench your thirst on a hot and humid day!










Ingredients


Juice

  • 2 cups coconut juice
  • 370 ml evaporated milk
  • 300 ml condensed milk
  • 1 cup coconut meat strips
  • ¼ teaspoon red food color

Gulaman

  • 1 tablespoon red unflavored jelly powder
  • 1 tablespoon green unflavored jelly powder
  • 1 tablespoon yellow unflavored jelly powder
  • 6 cups water
  • 6 tablespoons white sugar

Tapioca Pearl

  • ½ cup uncooked Tapioca pearls
  • 4 cups water

Procedures



For Gulaman


  1. Prepare each color. Remember to follow the jelly powder to the liquid ratio on the package as they vary depending on the brand.
  2. Per color, sprinkle the jelly powder over 2 cups of water. Add 2 tablespoons of sugar. Stir until dissolve.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil over low heat. Turn off the heat once it starts boiling and pour the gelatin mixture into seperate molds and refrigerate to set.
  4. Once they have set, cut into small cubes and set aside.


For Tapioco Pearl


  1. Boil 4 cups of water then add the tapioca pearls. Let it cook for 10 minutes or until the pearls are completely translucent. Drain and set aside



To Assemble Buko Salad Drink


  1. Pour the evaporated milk and condensed milk in a bowl and mix. Stir in the coconut juice until well blended. Add the coconut strips, tapioca pearls and gulaman cubes. Mix well.
  2. Drop ¼ teaspoon of red food color in the mixture and stir until it turns into an even pink color.
  3. Transfer in a pitcher and add ice cubes or refrigerate. it will give a bitter flavor. Allow it to cool for 20 minutes.
Guyabano Juice

GUYABANO JUICE






Guyabano juice is a popular and refreshing drink in the Philippines, known for its tropical flavor and health benefits. Made from the creamy, white flesh of the guyabano fruit (also known as soursop), this juice is often enjoyed chilled on hot days or served as a nutritious beverage year-round.






Ingredients


  • 1 large about 1 pound soft yet firm guyabano fruit
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • juice of 1 lime

Procedures


  1. Wash guyabano under cold running water. With a small knife, peel skin.
  2. Cut into half and remove middle rind. Remove seeds from flesh and discard.
  3. In a blender, combine the seeded flesh and 2 cups of the water. Process until pureed. In a fine mesh strainer, strain blended fruit and the remaining 1 cup water to remove fruit fibers.
  4. Add sugar and lime juice and stir until blended. Refrigerate to chill or serve cold over ice.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup sugar
  • 2 cups young coconut meat
  • 1 cup coconut juice
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • ice

Procedures


  1. In a saucepot, combine sugar and water. Over medium heat, bring to a simmer, stirring regularly, until sugar is dissolved and syrup is clear and no longer cloudy.

Tapuy

TAPUY





Tapuy is an indigenous rice wine produced in the northern provinces of the Philippines, particularly in the Cordillera regions. The native tribes produce this alcoholic beverage during special occasions such as weddings, fiestas, harvesting ceremonies, baptism, and as an offering to their deities or anitos.

Tanduay Rum

TANDUAY RUM




Tanduay Rum is a Filipino brand with more than 160 years of history. Today, it maintains its status as the Rum Brand Champion since Drinks International declared it as the world’s number #1 rum by volume of 2020 for three consecutive years. From the 1930s, the rum it produced began to be branded as “Tanduay Rhum”. The original distillery adopted its name from its first location on Isla de Tanduay, an area bordering the Quiapo and San Miguel districts in Manila. The aging, compounding, and bottling facilities are distributed in four plants located in elevated regions of the country.

Lambanog

LAMBANOG





Coconut wine or Lambanog, dubbed as the Philippine Vodka, is natural and chemical-free alcoholic liquor obtained from distillation of naturally fermented coconut sap. It is known for its high alcohol content and potency. It contains a minimum of 30% alcohol and can be used as base liquor to other flavored spirits and cocktail concoctions. It is an indigenous product to the Philippines and an integral part of the Filipinos’ festive heritage.

Laksoy

LAKSOY





Laksoy (also spelled lacsoy), is a traditional Filipino distilled nipa palm liquor. It is derived from tubâ (palm toddy) made from nipa palm sap that has been aged for at least 48 hours. It originates from Eastern Mindanao, the Visayas Islands (where it is known as dalisay or dalisay de nipa), and the Bicol Region (where it is known as barik).

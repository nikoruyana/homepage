CHICHIRYA

"Tsitsirya" in Filipino refers to small, crispy snacks typically eaten as appetizers or desserts. They are commonly sold in small packets or plastic bags and can be found in street-side stores, eateries, or roadside stalls. Tsitsirya can be sweet, salty, spicy, or sour, and they come in various forms, including chicharon (pork cracklings), peanuts, salted eggs, or any other snacks commonly available in the Philippines.

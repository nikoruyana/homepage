Chicken liver and gizzard adobo

Chicken Liver and Gizzard Adobo is a dish made with chicken liver and gizzard stewed in a sauce made with cane vinegar and soy sauce with lots of garlic. Mixing this two chicken parts can’t be tricky, one cooks easily and one cooks very long, one is soft then one is tough but one thing they both agree on is that they are a good candidate for an adobo dish as it gives flavour to the flavourless and it also accentuates the creamy taste.

Ingredients


  • 500 g chicken gizzard

  • 500 g chicken liver

  • 1 whole garlic, minced

  • ½ cup cane vinegar

  • ½ cup Philippine soy sauce

  • 1 tbsp sugar

  • oil

  • cornstarch

  • bay leaves

  • whole pepper corns

  • water

Instructions

  1. In a pot sauté garlic in oil using low heat until golden brown.
  2. Remove from pot and set aside.

  3. Add chicken liver and fry until browning occurs. Remove from pot then set it aside.

  4. Add chicken gizzard and fry until browning occurs.

  5. Add ½ cup water, ½ cup vinegar, ½ cup soy sauce, 2 tbsp peppercorn, 4 bay leaves and 1 tbsp sugar then bring to a boil and simmer for 30-35 minutes or until gizzard is tender.

  6. Mix 1 tsp cornstarch in 4 tablespoon water then add the mixture in the pot together with the fried liver.

  7. Add half of the fried garlic, simmer for additional 10 minutes, reduce the liquid further if needed then serve topped with the remaining fried garlic.

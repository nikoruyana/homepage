Igado

Filipino Cuisine

Prep Time : 10minutes

Cooking Time : 45minutes

Igado is a beloved Ilocano dish consisting of pork tenderloin and various pig innards like liver, kidney, and heart. It's regarded as one of the finest Filipino dishes, though acquiring a taste for it may vary among individuals.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lbs pork tenderloin sliced into 2-inch strips
  • 1/2 lbs pork liver sliced into 2-inch strips
  • 1/2 lbs pork kidney
  • 1 Knorr Pork Cube
  • 1 granulated white sugar
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 carrot julienne cut
  • 3 laurel leaves
  • 5 cloves garlic minced
  • 6 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 5 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 1/4 cup water
  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil
  • to taste salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Boil the kidney with 4 thumbs of ginger for at least 30 minutes. Let it cool down and slice into 2-inch strips. Set aside.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil in a pan. Sauté the pork liver for 2 minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Pour the remaining oil in the pan. Once hot, sauté garlic and onion until the latter softens. Add the pork kidney. Cook for 1 minute.
    Add the pork tenderloin. Sauté until the outer layer turns light brown. Pour the soy sauce and vinegar into the pan. Let the liquid boil. Stir.
  4. Add the laurel leaves and pour water. Let boil. Reduce the heat between low to medium setting. Continue cooking for 40 minutes.
  5. Add Knorr Pork Cube. Cook for 8 minutes.
  6. TAdd the liver back into the pan. Stir and continue cooking for 3 minutes.
  7. Put the bell pepper, carrot, and green peas.
  8. Season with salt and ground black pepper. Cook for 2 minutes.Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve hot with rice.

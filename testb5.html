Paksiw na Bangus

Paksiw na Bangus is one of the easiest and tastiest ways to cook milkfish! The fish is stewed in vinegar and spices and turns out super moist and flavorful. Pair it with steamed rice for a healthy, delicious, and budget-friendly meal to serve the whole family.

Relyenong Bangus

Relyenong bangus is more a special occasion dish than an everyday family dinner. Making this stuffed fish entails a very involved process and a tedious amount of work that is usually reserved for parties and celebrations.

Inihaw na Bangus

Milkfish stuffed with tomato and onions and grilled to perfection. This dish is best when served with a dipping sauce composed of soy sauce and lemon or calamansi. Enjoy it with warm white rice.

Bangus Sisig

Sisig has been one of the best creations in Filipino cuisine. It is delicious in many ways. There is also more than one way to make this dish. Bangus sisig is a good version that can be considered as an alternative to pork. It is healthier, yummy, and easy to prepare.

Ginataang Bangus

Ginataang Bangus is a Filipino dish of fish and vegetables cooked in coconut milk. Filipinos are familiar with the bangus (milkfish) variety, common in the Philippines, which has a thick, meaty texture.The addition of coconut milk at the end makes for a flavorful, and fragrant entree.

Fried Bangus

Fried Bangus or Fried milkfish is a simple dish that is packed with flavors. The milkfish alone has its deliciously rich taste (especially the belly) while the marinade gives it a sour spicy flavor.

Escabecheng Bangus

Escabecheng Bangus is a popular dish in the Philippines. This dish is not only easy to make but very delicious. Simply fry the fish and top it with the sweet and sour sauce like and serve.

Filipino Bangus Steak

Filipino Fish Steak makes use of boneless bangus belly. Bangus or milkfish is the national fish of the Philippines. I get my bangus belly from the Filipino store; it is already prepared this way – bones and scales are removed and all that I need to do is to slice the fish into serving pieces; this saves me a lot of time.

Sinigang na Bangus

In this Filipino fish recipe, bangus (milkfish) is stewed in a flavorful, slightly sour tamarind broth with lots of vegetables, an all-time favorite.

Adobong Bangus

Adobong Bangus is a delightful variation of the beloved Filipino adobo, where succulent milkfish takes center stage. This recipe infuses the mild, tender flesh of bangus with the classic adobo flavors of soy sauce and vinegar, resulting in a dish that’s both comforting and satisfying.
